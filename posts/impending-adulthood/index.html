When am I supposed to feel like an adult?

A week after a wonderful wedding reception, secluded in a tiny Colorado town for a miniature honeymoon, I asked my new wife an important question.

When are we supposed to feel like adults?

It hadn't happened yet. I thought the wedding might do it. There were vows, pictures, delicous cakes, and smiling faces. None of that resulted in a feeling of adulthood.

I certainly feel like I know "How to Adult". That training began early. I am an only child. I never really understood that I was a kid, because there weren't other models of identity available.

While I think that I do a passable job of impersonating an adult when people are watching, I definitely have imposter syndrome. I'm not afraid that someone will find out that I'm not an actual adult. That would be a relief. Adulting is a lot of work. It is exhuasting. I'm glad that I don't have to do it all the time.
