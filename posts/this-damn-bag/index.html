This Damn Bag

This Walgreen's plastic bag entered our lives during a particularly windy day last year.

It greets me every day. Sometimes waving. Sometimes more lethargic.

It has caused brainstorming sessions about possible ways to remove it. It has made me want to buy one of those expensive ladders from Costco. It has survived being pelted by rocks.

My wife thinks it mocks us. I think it gives us a gentle reminder about our place in the world.

Its humble indifference is a testament to the importance of acceptance.

It is a guerilla sculpture about the excesses of capitalism.

It is a challenge without a particularly compelling need for a solution.

It is a constant reminder of how nice it would be to have that expensive Costco ladder. I mean really for only a couple hundred bucks I could snag that bag and also dust those high up walls in our apartment. 30 minutes of manual labor bliss is a bargain at half the price.
