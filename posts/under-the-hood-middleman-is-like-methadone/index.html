Under the hood: Middleman is like Methadone

I am fascinated by content management systems. I have toyed around with: Wordpress, Movable Type, Drupal, Cascade, and others that I've forgotten. For work, I get to hack on Blackboard Learn and Moodle nearly every day. Years ago I even got to work on a cutting edge experimental scholarly digital publishing platform called Scalar. By "work on" I mean beta test and pitch a few wireframes to some very patient developers who then smiled, nodded, and patted me on the head. Check it out, the product is much better off without my suggestions.

I've even tried my hand at writing a few of my own. My efforts generally came to an end once my hack job zombie creation proved that it could shuffle.

Writing your own blogging engine is a good early programming task. It is very easy to be excited by it at the beginning. They are easy to understand and produce a couple of interesting wrinkles to code around. This practice has the romantic appeal of a carpenter making his own tools. It has a seductive allure. It is a lumbersexual-Protestant-work-ethic-pulling-yourself-up-by-your-bootstraps wrapper for staring at your belly button.

Staring at your belly button is fantastic. My mother confirms that I used to spend hours folded up investigating mine. At the right age, this narcissism is adorable and probably an important Freudian stage of development. I still think it is a great learning-to-program project, but it also probably needs to stop at some point.

I have spent countless hours fiddling with tools to amplify my voice, when I would have been better off working on things for that voice to say. At some point, preparation becomes procrastination.

I spent the better part of a week trying to figure out the perfect platform on which to run this site. I even considered diving into the world of graph databases just to stay current. In the end, I realized that what I wanted was to write every day. The writing was the focus on this particular project. Learning a new technology was not.

I drew some inspiration from this Sitepoint article by Benjamin Tan on Middleman.

Middleman has been a great way to start quickly and doesn't feel completely prepackaged either. I hope it breaks my cycle of technology-for-its-own-sake addiction by gently scratching my New Englander itch to build a site out of a tree I felled with my bare hands.

Middleman is like Methadone. (They did not use this tagline)
